Chest pain, or angina, can be a symptom of heart attack, as it can indicate a lack of oxygen to the heart. The normal range for chest pain is none. However, chest pain is considered abnormal when it is persistent or significantly interferes with daily activities.

Resting Blood Pressure

Resting blood pressure measures the force of blood against the walls of the arteries when the heart is at rest. The normal range for resting blood pressure is below 120/80 mm Hg. However, high blood pressure, or hypertension, is considered abnormal when the reading is consistently above 130/80 mm Hg.

Cholestoral

Cholesterol is a waxy, fat-like substance that can build up in the arteries and increase the risk of heart attack. The normal range for cholesterol is below 200 mg/dL. However, high levels of LDL cholesterol, also known as "bad" cholesterol, are considered abnormal when they are consistently above 120 mg/dL.

Fasting Blood Sugar

Fasting blood sugar measures the amount of glucose in the blood after a period of fasting. The normal range for fasting blood sugar is below 100 mg/dL. Resting ECG

A resting electrocardiogram, or ECG, measures the electrical activity of the heart at rest. The normal range for resting ECG results is a regular heart rhythm with no signs of abnormal activity. However, abnormal ECG results, such as irregular heart rhythms or abnormal waves, are considered abnormal and may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.

Heart Rate

Maximum heart rate achieved measures the highest heart rate reached during exercise. The normal range for maximum heart rate achieved varies depending on age and physical fitness level, but generally ranges from 220 minus age to 180 minus age. However, an abnormally high or low maximum heart rate achieved may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.

Induced Angina

Exercise-induced angina, or chest pain during exercise, can be a symptom of heart attack, as it can indicate a lack of oxygen to the heart. The normal range for exercise-induced angina is none. However, chest pain during exercise is considered abnormal and may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.

Old Peak

Old peak measures the amount of ST depression on the ECG during exercise. The normal range for old peak varies depending on individual factors such as age and physical fitness level, but an abnormally high or prolonged old peak may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.

SLP

Single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) is a diagnostic imaging test that can be used to assess blood flow to the heart. The normal range for SPECT results is a normal blood flow pattern with no signs of abnormalities. However, abnormal SPECT results, such as reduced blood flow to the heart, are considered abnormal and may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.

CAA

Coronary artery calcification (CAC) is a test that uses computed tomography (CT) scans to measure the amount of calcium buildup in the arteries of the heart. The normal range for CAC scores is zero, indicating no calcium buildup. However, higher CAC scores are considered abnormal and may indicate an increased risk of heart attack.
