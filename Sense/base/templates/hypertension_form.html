Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that can affect the heart, such as coronary artery disease, heart rhythm disorders, and heart defects. The presence of heart disease is a significant risk factor for hypertension. There is no specific numerical range for heart disease, but it can be diagnosed through medical tests and examinations.
If you have any heart disease, please enter 1 or else enter 0.

Glucose Level

Glucose is a type of sugar that the body uses for energy. High glucose levels can indicate the presence of diabetes, which is a major risk factor for hypertension. The normal range for fasting glucose levels is between 70-100 mg/dL, and levels above 126 mg/dL are considered diabetic.

BMI

BMI, or body mass index, is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. A high BMI is a significant risk factor for hypertension. A healthy BMI range is between 18.5-24.9 kg/m², while a BMI of 25-29.9 kg/m² is considered overweight, and a BMI of 30 kg/m² or higher is considered obese.

Heart Stroke

A stroke occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, either by a blockage or a burst blood vessel. Hypertension is a significant risk factor for stroke. There is no specific numerical range for stroke, but it can be diagnosed through medical tests and examinations.
If you have had a stroke in the past, enter 1 or else enter 0.
