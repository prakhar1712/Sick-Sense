Polydipsia

Polydipsia is a medical condition characterized by excessive thirst and increased fluid intake. In the context of diabetes, it may indicate high blood glucose levels. The normal range for thirst varies, but if you notice an increase in your usual thirst levels, it may be worth discussing with your healthcare provider. An abnormal range for thirst can be subjective and varies depending on the individual. However, if you are consuming more than 3-4 liters of fluids per day or experiencing constant thirst, you should speak to your doctor.

Sudden weight Loss

Sudden weight loss can be a symptom of diabetes, as the body may begin to break down fat and muscle tissue for energy if it is not able to properly use glucose. The normal range for weight loss varies depending on individual factors such as diet and exercise, but a sudden and significant loss of weight (such as 5% or more of body weight within a few months) could be cause for concern. A sustained weight loss of greater than 10% of the initial body weight warrants medical attention.

Partial Paresis

Partial paresis, or weakness or paralysis of a part of the body, may be a sign of nerve damage caused by high blood glucose levels in people with diabetes. The normal range for muscle strength varies depending on the muscle group and individual, but weakness or paralysis that persists over time should be evaluated by a healthcare provider. Paresis is considered abnormal when it is persistent or progressive.

Irritability

Irritability is a common symptom of diabetes, as high blood glucose levels can affect mood and energy levels. The normal range for irritability varies depending on individual factors such as stress and sleep, but if you notice significant changes in your mood or energy levels, it may be worth discussing with your healthcare provider. Irritability is considered abnormal if it is persistent or significantly interferes with daily activities.

Polyphagia

Polyphagia is a medical condition characterized by excessive hunger and increased food intake. In the context of diabetes prediction, it may indicate high blood glucose levels. The normal range for hunger varies, but if you notice an increase in your usual hunger levels, it may be worth discussing with your healthcare provider. Polyphagia is considered abnormal when it is persistent or significantly interferes with daily activities.

Visual blurring

Visual blurring, or blurred vision, may be a sign of high blood glucose levels affecting the lens of the eye. The normal range for visual acuity varies depending on individual factors such as age and eye health, but any changes in vision should be evaluated by an eye doctor or healthcare provider. Visual blurring is considered abnormal if it is persistent or significantly interferes with daily activities.
