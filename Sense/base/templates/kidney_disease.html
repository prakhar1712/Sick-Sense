Blood Pressure

Blood pressure is a measure of the force of blood against the walls of the arteries. High blood pressure, or hypertension, is a significant risk factor for kidney disease. Normal blood pressure is considered to be less than 120/80 mm Hg, while blood pressure between 120-139/80-89 mm Hg is considered pre-hypertension, and blood pressure above 140/90 mm Hg is considered hypertension.

RBC

RBC, or red blood cell count, is a measure of the number of red blood cells in the blood. Abnormal RBC counts can indicate a range of kidney diseases. The normal range for RBC count in men is between 4.5-5.5 million cells/mcL, while the normal range for women is between 4.0-5.0 million cells/mcL.

Hypertension

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a significant risk factor for kidney disease. Normal blood pressure is considered to be less than 120/80 mm Hg, while blood pressure above 140/90 mm Hg is considered hypertension.

Diabetic

Diabetes is a chronic condition characterized by high levels of glucose in the blood. Diabetes is a significant risk factor for kidney disease. The normal range for fasting glucose levels is between 70-100 mg/dL, and levels above 126 mg/dL are considered diabetic.
Enter 1 if you are diabetic or else enter 0.

CAD

CAD, or coronary artery disease, is a condition in which the arteries that supply blood to the heart become narrowed or blocked, leading to reduced blood flow and oxygen to the heart muscle. CAD is a significant risk factor for kidney disease.
If you have coronary arterey disease, enter 1 or else enter 0.

Appetite

Poor appetite or loss of appetite is a common symptom of kidney disease. It can be caused by a buildup of waste products in the blood or by changes in hormones that control appetite. An evaluation of appetite can provide useful information in predicting kidney disease.
Enter 1 for a good appetie ar else enter 0 for a bad appetite.

Protein Evaluation

Proteinuria, or the presence of excess protein in the urine, is a significant indicator of kidney disease. A protein evaluation test measures the amount of protein in the urine. The normal range for proteinuria is less than 150 mg/24 hours.
Enter 1 if proteinuria is above 150 mg, or else enter 0.

Anemia

Anemia is a condition in which there are not enough red blood cells to carry oxygen to the body's tissues. Anemia is a common complication of kidney disease. A blood test to measure hemoglobin levels can help diagnose anemia. The normal range for hemoglobin in men is between 13.5-17.5 g/dL, while the normal range for women is between 12.0-15.5 g/dL.
